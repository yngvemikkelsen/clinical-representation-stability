================================================================================
EXPECTED PAPER RESULTS
================================================================================

With all 4 groups analyzed:

METHODS:
--------
"We tested representation quality across four conditions: (1) real
clinical texts from the MT samples corpus varying in complexity and
length (n=450); (2) synthetically generated texts matched for complexity
and length (n=450); (3) length-controlled synthetic texts with matched
length but varying complexity (n=300); (4) length-controlled real texts
with matched length but varying complexity (n=300). This design separates
complexity effects from sequence length while validating findings across
data sources."

RESULTS:
--------
"Collapse patterns replicated across real and synthetic texts (r=0.94,
p<0.001). Length-controlled analyses revealed that complexity drives
collapse independent of sequence length: within matched-length groups,
complex content showed significantly greater collapse than simple content
(synthetic: d=2.8, p<0.001; real: d=2.6, p<0.001). Effects were robust
across both synthetic and real clinical texts."

DISCUSSION:
-----------
"The convergence of findings across real clinical texts, synthetic texts,
and length-controlled conditions demonstrates that representation collapse
is driven by clinical information density rather than sequence length,
and that this effect generalizes robustly across data sources."
